# Security Awareness Training

## Overview

Security awareness training helps users recognize, prevent, and report security risks. Training should be role-appropriate, practical, and measurable.

## Topics

- Phishing and social engineering.
- Passwords and MFA.
- Safe handling of sensitive data.
- Secure remote work.
- Device and patch hygiene.
- Reporting suspicious activity.
- Cloud and SaaS sharing risks.
- Secure use of AI tools.
- Incident response basics.

## Audience Segments

- General staff.
- Developers and engineers.
- Finance and HR.
- Executives.
- IT and operations.
- Customer support and sales.

## Training Design

1. Define target audience and risk scenarios.
2. Choose behaviors to change.
3. Use realistic examples from the organization's context.
4. Keep lessons short and actionable.
5. Include reporting procedures and escalation contacts.
6. Measure outcomes with quizzes, simulations, or incident metrics.
7. Update content after incidents and threat changes.

## Exercise Ideas

- Identify phishing indicators in sample emails.
- Practice reporting a suspicious message.
- Review data classification examples.
- Spot unsafe file sharing.
- Walk through MFA fatigue attack response.
- Discuss secure handling of customer data.

## Metrics

- Completion rate.
- Quiz score improvement.
- Phishing simulation reporting rate.
- Reduction in repeat risky behavior.
- Time to report incidents.
- Policy acknowledgment.

## Deliverables

- Training outline.
- Slide or script content.
- Hands-on exercise.
- Quiz/checklist.
- Follow-up reinforcement plan.
